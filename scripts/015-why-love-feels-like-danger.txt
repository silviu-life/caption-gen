Love should feel like safety. But for some of us, it feels like standing at the edge of a cliff. The closer someone gets, the louder the alarm screams. Not because this person is dangerous. But because your body learned, long before you had words for it, that closeness leads to devastation. Maybe you opened your heart once and what came back was silence. Or someone choosing another person over you. Now every time intimacy approaches, your system braces, scans, pulls back just enough to survive what it is sure is coming. Carl Jung understood that we fall in love not only with a person but with what they awaken in us. When what they awaken is an old wound, love and danger become the same feeling. The healing begins when you see that the danger is not about them. It is a memory, replaying. What is the oldest memory your body carries about love?
