Have you ever wondered why the kindest people sometimes carry the most resentment? A person learns early that love is conditional. That the only way to keep people close is to make yourself useful, agreeable, small. So they become the giver. The one who abandons their own needs the moment someone else has a request. And underneath, something keeps a precise record. Every sacrifice. Every time they said yes when their body screamed no. Jung understood this. Anything pushed far enough flips into its opposite. The people-pleaser does not simply burn out. They transform. The warmth becomes withdrawal. The person who gave everything has nothing left. This is not cruelty. It is the cost of abandoning yourself to keep others comfortable. The answer is not to stop being kind. It is to stop being kind at your own expense. If this resonated, follow for more.
