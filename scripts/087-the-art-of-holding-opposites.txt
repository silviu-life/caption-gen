One of the most difficult things a human being can do is hold two contradictory truths at the same time without choosing sides. You can love someone deeply and know they are not right for you. You can be proud of the life you built and grieve the one you did not live. You can be strong and still need to be held. Most of us were never taught this. We were taught to pick a side. Good or bad. Strong or weak. And so we spend our lives in an exhausting war with ourselves, trying to eliminate one half of who we are so the other half can feel safe. Jung called the ability to hold opposites the transcendent function. Not someone who has resolved their contradictions. But someone who has stopped trying to. Because the tension between opposites is not a problem. It is the raw material of transformation. When you can hold darkness and light in the same hand, something new emerges. Not a compromise. Something that could not exist if you had chosen one side and abandoned the other. The people who seem most at peace are not the ones with answers. They are the ones who learned to live gracefully inside questions. What contradictions in yourself have you been trying to resolve instead of hold?
