You did not come into this world wearing a mask. You built one. Carefully, over years, with every rejection, every time someone said you were too much or not enough. Jung called it the Persona, the face you show the world so the world will let you stay. But here is what no one tells you. Behind the mask lives everything you were told to hide. Your intensity. Your anger. Your hunger for something real. Jung called that the Shadow. And it does not disappear just because you stopped showing it. It waits. It watches through the eyeholes of the mask you built. And it grows louder the longer you pretend it is not there. The question is not whether you have a shadow. The question is how long you plan to keep pretending the mask is your real face. What part of yourself did you hide to be accepted?
