Some of us grew up in homes where silence was the safest language. Where having needs made you a burden. Where the only way to survive was to make yourself small and invisible. Jung observed that the child who is not allowed to express anger does not become a peaceful adult. They become someone who does not understand why they explode over nothing. Why a small frustration makes them see red. Why someone leaving a message unread sends them into a darkness far too large for the situation. That is not a flaw. That is decades of swallowed fire finding cracks in the surface. The rage you carry now may not belong to your present life. It may belong to a child who once sat in a room, alone, learning that the world did not want to hear what they feel. The answer is not to suppress the fire again. It is to finally listen to what it has been trying to say. Save this for when you need it. Follow for more.
