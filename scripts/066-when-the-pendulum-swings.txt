Why does the person who escapes a rigid life so often fall into chaos? Why does the one who spent years being selfless suddenly turn cold? Jung called this enantiodromia. Whatever you push to one extreme will swing to the other. Not because you are broken. Because the psyche demands balance. Think of a pendulum held to one side for years. The moment you release it, it does not return gently. It swings violently the other way. The faith you suppressed becomes cynicism. The emotions you buried become outbursts you cannot explain. This is not failure. It is the cost of living too far from your center. The healing is in shortening the arc. In learning to live closer to the middle, where neither extreme runs your life. Follow if you have ever wondered how you became the thing you swore you would never be.
