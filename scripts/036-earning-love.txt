You were not born believing you had to earn love. That was taught. Somewhere in childhood the message arrived. Maybe through a parent who only softened when you performed. Maybe through a silence that followed your tears. And so you began the work. Becoming kinder, smarter, more useful. Less yourself. Jung called this the false self. Built entirely around what others need, with nothing left for who you actually are. The exhaustion you feel is not from life. It is from constantly proving you deserve to be in it. But Jung also said that the privilege of a lifetime is to become who you truly are. And that begins the moment you stop performing your worth and start believing it was there all along.
