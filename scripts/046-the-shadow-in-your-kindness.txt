Have you ever met someone who is relentlessly kind but never sets a single boundary. Who smiles through situations that should make them furious. Jung would see the Shadow at work. Because sometimes niceness is not a virtue. It is a hiding place. When you reject your capacity for anger, your directness, your ability to say no with your whole body, those things do not vanish. They go underground. They come out as resentment, as control disguised as care. The person who cannot be direct ends up being dishonest. Not because they are bad. Because the part of them that could draw a line was thrown away long ago. Gentleness without backbone is not kindness. It is submission wearing a kind face. Real kindness comes from someone who could say no and chooses to say yes. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
