Your body remembers what your mind has tried to forget. There are wounds from childhood that never became stories. They became sensations instead. The tightness in your chest when someone raises their voice. The heaviness in your stomach when the phone goes quiet. The way your legs go weak when you sense someone pulling away. Carl Jung understood that the psyche and the body are not separate. When a child experiences something too overwhelming to process, the body holds it. Not as a memory. As a reflex that fires decades later in situations that look nothing like the original wound but feel exactly the same. Every time you freeze when you should speak. Every time you shut down when someone gets close. That is a child's wisdom, still running its oldest program. Healing does not always begin with understanding. Sometimes it begins with placing a hand over the place that hurts and saying, I know. I see you. What does your body remember that your mind has forgotten?
