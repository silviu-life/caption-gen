Some of the most wounded people you will ever meet are the ones who never stop helping others. They will hold your pain at three in the morning. They will give until there is nothing left. And they will never once turn that tenderness toward themselves. Jung would recognize this immediately. The compulsive helper is often running from their own shadow. As long as you are focused on someone else's wounds, you never have to sit with your own. Underneath the helper's identity is a child who learned the only way to keep people close was to be useful. Not loved for who they are. Needed for what they do. The shadow of the helper is the abandoned one who never learned to say, I need help too. What would change if you stopped rescuing others long enough to rescue yourself?
