For most of your life you have been taught that the dark parts of yourself are the enemy. That anger is dangerous. That the hungry, restless thing inside you needs to be controlled or prayed away. Carl Jung spent decades proving the opposite. The Shadow is not your enemy. It is your estranged ally. It carries everything you were forced to abandon in order to survive your childhood, your family, your culture. And it has been waiting, not to destroy you, but to be invited back into the house. Jung would say what you resist persists. The anger you refuse to feel becomes depression. The grief you will not touch becomes anxiety. The power you are afraid to claim becomes the very weakness you hate in yourself. But the moment you stop fighting your shadow and start listening to it, the war inside you begins to end. Not because you become darker. Because you finally become complete. The shadow does not want to take over. It wants to come home. And welcoming it back is perhaps the bravest thing a human being can do. Jung called this individuation. The process of becoming whole. Not by being perfect. By being all of yourself. What would change if you stopped treating your darkness as the enemy and started treating it as the missing piece?
