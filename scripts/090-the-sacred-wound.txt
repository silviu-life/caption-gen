Some wounds do not heal the way you expect. They do not close and disappear. Instead, they become the very place where light enters you. Jung noticed something remarkable. The wound that nearly destroyed a person often became the source of their greatest gift. The child who was never heard grows into the adult who listens like no one else can. The one who knew loneliness becomes the person who sits with someone else's pain without flinching. The heart that was shattered learns a tenderness an unbroken heart could never know. This is not about glorifying suffering. It is about recognizing that meaning can grow in the exact place where meaning was taken. Jung called the numinous a moment when something beyond the ordinary touches your life. And often that touch comes through the crack the wound left behind.
