There is a particular suffering that belongs to people who spent their lives being good. They follow rules. They put others first. And underneath, a quiet rage they have never been allowed to feel. Jung understood that compulsive goodness is often not virtue. It is survival. The child who learned love was conditional, that anger meant abandonment, did not choose to be good. They chose to be safe. The prison is invisible because the world rewards it. People admire your selflessness. They have no idea that what they see is not generosity, but fear. Fear that if you stop being useful you will be discarded. Jung called this persona inflation, when the mask grows so thick you lose the human beneath it. The way out is not to become bad. It is to become real. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
