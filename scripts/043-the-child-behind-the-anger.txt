Behind every flash of rage there is almost always a child who was never allowed to cry. Jung understood this. When a child learns that sadness is not safe, that tears are met with silence or punishment, the grief does not disappear. It puts on armor. The adult who erupts over small things, who carries a fire they cannot explain, is often protecting a child who was never given permission to hurt. If you find yourself burning over nothing, do not ask what is wrong with you. Ask what is underneath. What were you never allowed to feel?
