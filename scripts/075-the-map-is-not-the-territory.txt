Have you ever considered that the person you think you are might be the smallest fraction of who you actually are? Jung drew a line between the ego and the Self. The ego is the story you tell about yourself. It is useful. But it is a map, not the territory. The territory contains everything you have forgotten. Every capacity you abandoned because it did not fit the role you were given. We mistake the map for the whole because the ego is loud and the Self is quiet. The Self whispers in dreams, in moments of awe, in the knowing that arrives when you stop thinking. Most people live inside the map. They optimize it, defend it. And wonder why something still feels missing. What would change if you stopped defending your idea of yourself long enough to meet who you actually are?
