The performance was only supposed to be temporary. You started it to survive something. A family that only loved you when you were useful. A world that only seemed safe when you had the right answers. So you built a version of yourself that could handle all of it. Confident on the outside. Always reading the room. Jung called this Persona identification — the moment when the mask grows into the face. When the actor forgets they are acting. People admire the performance. They trust the version of you that never stumbles. But inside, something is crouching in a corner, exhausted, wondering when it will be allowed to stop. That part is not your weakness. It is your realness. The mask kept you safe. But the person behind it kept you alive. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
