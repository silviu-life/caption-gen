There is a wound in your past that is still making decisions for you today. Jung spent decades studying how old pain reorganizes the psyche. He called these reorganizations complexes, and he found they operate like invisible hands on the steering wheel of your life. You think you are choosing freely. But the complex is choosing for you. Here is how it works. A child experiences something too painful to process. Perhaps they gave everything to a dream and it ended in humiliation. The psyche does what it does under unbearable pressure. It splits. It takes the passionate, trusting part and locks it away. Not out of cruelty. Out of mercy. The logic is devastating. If trying with everything you have leads to that kind of pain, then the safest thing is to never try with everything again. And so a pattern is born. You hold back at work. You choose partners who are safe rather than the ones who move you. You abandon things right before they succeed. None of this is conscious. Jung said what remains unconscious comes to you from the outside and you call it fate. But it is not fate. It is a wound that never finished healing. Part two will explore how to begin.
