Some of the heaviest things you carry were never yours to begin with. They were handed to you in a house where no one said what was really going on. A father who modeled that life is exhaustion. A mother whose love came with conditions you could never meet. A family where having needs made you a burden and happiness felt like betrayal. Jung called this the family complex, the invisible loyalty that shapes you before you can question it. You did not choose these loyalties. You absorbed them through silence and watching. And now you may still be living inside someone else's story. Still waiting for permission from someone who could never give it. The most radical thing you may ever do is ask — which parts of my life are mine, and which am I carrying for someone who never asked me to? Follow to continue the journey inward.
