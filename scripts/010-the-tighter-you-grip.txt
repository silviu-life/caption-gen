The tighter you grip, the more you lose. This is not a metaphor. It is a law of the psyche. Jung called it enantiodromia, the principle that anything pushed to an extreme becomes its opposite. The person who controls every outcome creates the very chaos they fear. The one who refuses to let go squeezes the love right out of it. We grip because we are afraid. But the grip itself drives away what we are trying to hold. There is a strange kind of strength in the open hand. Not defeat. Just trusting that what is yours does not need to be forced. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
