No one ever became whole without first being broken open. This is one of the hardest truths Jung offered. We are taught that suffering is a problem to solve. That pain is a sign you are doing something wrong. That the goal of life is to arrange things so carefully that nothing ever hurts. But Jung saw something different in the people who sat across from him. The ones cracked open by loss, by failure, by the collapse of everything they believed about themselves. Those were the ones who had the chance to become real. Not because suffering is noble. But because suffering strips away the false self. It removes the armor that was never really protecting you. It reveals what no amount of success ever could. There is a pain that arrives when the life you built stops working. When the role you have been playing no longer fits. When everything should feel fine and instead you feel hollowed out. That is not failure. That is your deeper self, what Jung called the Self, demanding to be heard. The wound is where the light enters. Not because pain is good. But because pain is honest. And honesty is the only doorway to the life that is actually yours. What has your suffering been trying to show you?
