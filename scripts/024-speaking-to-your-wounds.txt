Most people try to heal their wounds by fighting them. They push through the feeling. They treat the broken part like an enemy to defeat. But Jung discovered something that changed everything. The wounded part does not need to be conquered. It needs to be spoken to. He called this active imagination, the practice of turning inward and meeting the parts that carry pain. Not analyzing them. Just sitting with them. The way you would sit with a frightened child who has been hiding alone for years. You would not lecture that child. You would get on the floor and say I see you, I am here, I am not leaving. The wound does not disappear. But it softens. It stops running your life from the shadows. Healing is not a war against your broken places. It is a conversation with them. Save this for the day you need it.
