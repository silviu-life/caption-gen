What if the parent you have been waiting for your entire life is the person you are becoming right now? Jung believed that inside every adult there lives a child who still carries the original wound. Not the wound of what happened to them, but the wound of what never happened. The words that were never spoken. The comfort that never came. The moment someone should have knelt down, looked them in the eyes, and said, this was not your fault. Most of us spend decades searching for that missing piece in other people. In partners who feel like home. In friends who make us feel like we finally belong. But something shifts when you stop looking outward and turn toward that child yourself. You do not need to be perfect to do this. You simply need to be willing to offer yourself what was never given. A hand on the chest when fear arrives. A gentle voice that says, I am here, even when you have been bad, even when you have failed, even when the world feels like too much. Jung called the process of becoming whole individuation. And part of that process, perhaps the most sacred part, is learning to parent yourself. Not with the harshness you inherited. But with the kindness you always deserved. The child in you is still listening. What do they need to hear from you today?
