There is a particular wound that comes not from what a parent did but from what they never gave. Sometimes they were in the room but never truly present. Jung spoke of the father archetype as the inner image of guidance and permission to enter the world. When that image is wounded, the child freezes. They grow up still waiting. For someone to say the words never spoken. I see you. I am proud of you. Because that permission never came, they spend years trying to earn it from bosses, partners, achievements that never fill the space. But the permission you seek does not have to come from them. You can give it to yourself. The child inside needs those words from the one person who will never leave. That person is you. What would change if you stopped waiting and gave yourself permission?
