Can you love someone without losing yourself in the process? For many of us, the answer has always been no. When we fall in love, something takes over. We stop doing what matters. We pour every ounce of attention outward and wonder why we feel empty. Jung would call this a failure of individuation. Wholeness does not mean being alone. It means remaining yourself even beside someone you love deeply. When you redirect all your love outward and leave nothing for yourself, the part of you that was growing begins to starve. That exhaustion is not the cost of loving someone. It is the cost of leaving yourself behind. Healthy love is not sacrifice. It is overflow. You fill your own well first, and what spills over is what you give. Not from emptiness. From the quiet fullness of someone who has not forgotten who they are. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
