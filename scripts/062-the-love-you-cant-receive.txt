The love you want most is often the love you block most fiercely. Someone offers tenderness and something in your chest tightens. They say they are not going anywhere and a voice inside whispers not yet. You watch their kindness the way a soldier watches an open field. This is not distrust. It is a wound that learned to protect itself by never fully receiving. Jung spoke of the Anima and Animus as bridges to the soul. When that bridge was burned in childhood, when love came with conditions or disappeared without warning, it does not rebuild just because someone trustworthy arrives. You cross it one honest moment at a time. Not by forcing your walls down. By letting one small thing land. A kind word you do not deflect. A gesture you do not explain away. Each time you let love reach you, something ancient begins to soften. Follow to continue the work of becoming whole.
