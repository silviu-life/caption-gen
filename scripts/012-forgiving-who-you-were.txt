Can you forgive the person you were before you understood what you were doing? This is one of the hardest questions a person can face. The mind wants to punish. It replays every mistake and whispers you should have known better. But Jung saw something different. He saw that most harm comes not from malice but from unconsciousness. From patterns inherited and never examined. From wounds driving behavior long before awareness arrived. The person you were at twenty was not making free choices. They were running programs written in childhood. And the cruelty you direct at your past self does not make you more moral. It keeps you chained to the very version of yourself you are trying to leave behind. Individuation asks something radical. It asks you to look at who you were with the same compassion you would offer a stranger in pain. Not to excuse what happened. But to see that healing cannot begin while you are at war with your own history. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
