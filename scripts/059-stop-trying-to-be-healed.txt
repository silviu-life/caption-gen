There is a quiet tyranny in the idea that you need to be healed before you are allowed to live. As if wholeness is a finish line. Jung never promised that. The Self does not ask you to be flawless. It asks you to stop pretending. Your sadness is not a sign the work has failed. Your anger is not evidence of a flaw you have not fixed. These are parts of your wholeness waiting to be included. We have turned healing into another performance. You do not need to be finished to be worthy. What would happen if you let yourself be exactly where you are right now?
