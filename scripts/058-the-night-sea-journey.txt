There is a descent that every person must make if they are serious about becoming who they actually are. The ancients called it the night sea journey. Jung called it the nekyia. It is the passage into your own darkness, not to be destroyed, but to retrieve something precious that can only be found there. Most people will do anything to avoid this descent. They stay busy. They stay numb. They rearrange the furniture of their lives and call it change. But the descent comes for you. It arrives as the depression with no obvious cause. The relationship that collapses despite your best efforts. The career that suddenly feels like a prison you built with your own hands. In that dark water, everything you believed about yourself comes apart. The titles, the roles, the identity you spent decades constructing. It all dissolves. This is terrifying. But Jung saw it as necessary. Because only when the old self has been dismantled can the deeper self emerge. The one that was there before you learned to perform. The night sea journey is not punishment. It is initiation. If you are in that dark water now, know this. You are not lost. You are on your way home. Save this for when you need it. Follow for more.
