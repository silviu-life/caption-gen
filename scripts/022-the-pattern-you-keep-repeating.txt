Why do you keep finding yourself in the same situation with different people? Different face, same feeling. Different relationship, same ending. Jung had a name for this. He called it repetition compulsion. The psyche's relentless need to return to what is unfinished. Not because you enjoy suffering. But because something in you is still trying to solve a puzzle set in childhood. The partner who goes quiet when you need them most. You have met them before. In the body of a parent who withdrew when you reached for them. We do not attract these patterns because we are broken. We attract them because the psyche is trying to complete something. Returning you to the scene of the wound, hoping this time you will respond differently. The pattern will not stop until you see it. And you cannot see it until you ask the most uncomfortable question. What am I re-creating? Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
