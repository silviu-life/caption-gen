There was a moment in childhood when you learned to hide. You may not remember it. But your body does. You reached out with something real — your joy, your need — and what came back was not welcome. Silence. Or the withdrawal of love that taught you more than words ever could. In that moment, a decision was made. Not by your thinking mind. By something deeper. This part of me is not safe to show. Jung understood that the persona forms in childhood, in the gap between who we are and what our world can receive. It hardens until we forget there was ever a face beneath it. The tragedy is not that we wear masks. It is that we forget we are wearing them. And the child who learned to hide stays hidden. Still waiting to hear what no one said. Follow to continue the journey inward.
