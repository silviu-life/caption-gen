Some people never arrive because they never stop trying to become. One book to the next. One workshop to the next. One breakthrough to the next wound. They call it growth. Jung would call it avoidance. As long as you are becoming, you never face the terrifying simplicity of just being. Being who you are now. With wounds still open. The ego loves transformation because it keeps the ego in charge. Always another layer. Always a reason to believe the real you is ahead, waiting at the end of enough effort. But the real you is not in the future. The real you is the one exhausted from looking. Jung called this ego inflation. Even growth becomes avoidance of surrender. The most courageous thing you may ever do is stop. Because you were never going somewhere. You were always coming home. What would happen if you let yourself arrive?
