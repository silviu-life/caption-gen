Shame disguises itself as conscience. That is its greatest trick. It tells you the weight you carry is proof you are a good person. That stopping the self-punishment would mean you do not care. Jung saw through this. He understood that shame is not morality. It is a wound pretending to be a virtue. And the person who carries it like a banner is not becoming better. They are frozen between who they were and who they could become. Nothing is protected by your suffering. The past does not change because you stay loyal to its pain. The moment you set shame down, you do not lose your conscience. You gain the freedom to actually change. Like this and follow if the unconscious speaks to you.
