Sometimes life arranges itself with a precision no plan could have produced. A failure pushes you onto a road you never would have chosen, and years later you see it was the only road that could have brought you here. Jung called synchronicity the evidence of a deeper order. Not fate written in stone. Something more alive. A conversation between your inner world and the outer, in a language older than logic. It does not happen because you earned it. It happens because something inside you reached a threshold. A readiness. The world responds not to your effort but to your alignment. Many of us have had one such moment. One coincidence so impossibly timed it rearranged how we understood our own life. We never talk about it because we cannot explain it. But we never forget it either. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
