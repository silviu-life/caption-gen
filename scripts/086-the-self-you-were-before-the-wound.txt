Do you remember who you were before the world told you who to be? Before the first rejection taught you to hide. Before you learned to dim yourself to fit inside rooms that were never built for someone your size. Jung believed every person is born with an original wholeness he called the Self. Not the self you constructed to survive. Not the version that performs for love. Something deeper. The part of you that existed before the wound, before the armor, before the mask. We spend the first half of life building walls to protect that self from a world that felt unsafe. And we spend the second half wondering why we feel so far from home. The journey back is not about becoming someone new. It is about remembering someone ancient. The child who laughed without checking the room first. Who loved without calculating the cost. That part of you did not die. It went into hiding. And it is waiting for you to say, you can come out now. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
