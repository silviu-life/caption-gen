Why does the person you love most trigger you the deepest? Not because they are doing something wrong. But because they are standing exactly where your oldest wounds live. Jung called this projection. We carry an inner image of love shaped by our earliest experiences of closeness and project it onto our partner without knowing it. When they pull away, we do not see someone who needs space. We see the parent who went silent. When they forget something small, we feel the childhood proof that our needs do not matter. Your partner is not your wound. But they stand in front of the mirror that reflects it. That is why intimate relationships are the most powerful place for healing. Not because the other person fixes you. Because they show you what still needs your attention. The question is not why do they trigger me? It is what inside me is asking to be seen. What old wound is your partner reflecting back to you?
