Some of the deepest patterns you carry were never yours to begin with. They were inherited. Passed down like heirlooms no one asked for. Jung called this the mother complex, and he did not mean it as blame. He meant it as recognition. The first person who shaped your understanding of love also shaped your understanding of what love costs. If your mother gave herself away to keep the peace, you may have learned that love means losing yourself. If her approval only came when you performed, you may still believe your worth lives in your output. If she was overwhelmed by her own pain, you may have learned to make yourself small so there was room for hers. These are not flaws. They are emotional inheritances. And the parent who passed them down was usually carrying an inheritance of their own. Breaking the pattern does not mean blaming the one who gave it to you. It means being the first one honest enough to say, this was never mine to carry. What did you inherit that you are ready to set down?
