There is a child inside you who stopped asking for love a long time ago. Not because they stopped wanting it. But because every time they reached out, what came back was silence, or conditions, or the message that who they are is not quite enough. Jung called this the wounded child archetype, and he believed it lives in every adult who learned too early that love must be earned. So that child did something clever. They stopped reaching. They built a life around proving their worth instead. Working harder. Performing better. Becoming whatever the room seemed to need. But the aching never left. It just went underground. That child is still waiting. Not for someone to rescue them. They are waiting for you. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
