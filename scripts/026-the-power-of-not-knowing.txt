What if the bravest thing you could do is admit you do not know? We grip so tightly to our need to be right. Jung called this the ego, the part that believes it must have every answer or everything falls apart. But the ego is a small room. The soul is the whole house. When you loosen your grip, something larger moves through you. Not chaos. A spaciousness the clenched mind could never hold. The open hand receives what the fist never could. Surrendering the need to be right is not giving up. It is trusting you were never meant to carry it alone. Follow to continue the journey inward.
