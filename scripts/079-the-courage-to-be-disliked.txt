The most radical thing you can do in a world that rewards conformity is to be yourself without apology. Jung understood that individuation requires a willingness to be disliked. The moment you stop shaping yourself for others, some will leave. And the ones who leave were never there for you. They were there for the performance. This is why authenticity feels like loss before freedom. You spent years curating a version that earns approval. Letting go means losing that approval. But it was never nourishing you. It was feeding the mask. The people who stay, who see you bare and choose to remain, those are the only relationships that will ever hold you. The courage to be disliked is the quiet refusal to abandon yourself one more time for the comfort of being accepted. What would change if you stopped needing everyone to approve?
