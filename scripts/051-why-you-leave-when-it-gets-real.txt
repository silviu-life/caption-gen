Why do you pull away the moment love starts to feel real. Not when things are bad. When things are good. When someone sees you without armor and still stays. That is when your system sounds the alarm. Jung understood that the deepest terror is not rejection. It is being fully known and then losing what you were given. So you find the exit before the floor drops. You cool your warmth. You tell yourself it was never going to last. But underneath that protection is something unbearably simple. A heart that wants to say I love you, please stay, and please do not hurt me. That is the sentence beneath every withdrawal. Not complexity. Just a human being asking for the simplest thing in the world. The flight response is not cowardice. It is a wound dressed as wisdom. What would it take to stay one moment longer than your fear says is safe?
