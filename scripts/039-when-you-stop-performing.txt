What happens when you stop performing? Not what you fear will happen. What actually happens? Most of us carry a terror that if we stop being useful or strong, people will leave. So we keep showing up as the version that earns love rather than the one that simply is. Jung called this the persona. He warned that those who merge too completely with their mask lose themselves entirely. There is an exhaustion from performing for years. The tiredness no sleep can cure. The only remedy is letting someone see you without the costume. Many go through life without risking that moment. And wonder why they feel lonely in rooms full of people who love them. That loneliness is not because they are unloved. It is because they are loved for someone they are not. What would you discover if you stopped performing long enough to find out?
