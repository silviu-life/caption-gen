You carry more than your own memories. Jung believed that beneath the personal unconscious there is something far older. He called it the collective unconscious. A shared layer of the psyche belonging to every human who has ever lived. This is why a child who has never seen a snake can feel terror at the sight of one. Why myths separated by oceans tell the same stories. The hero descending into darkness. The wise figure at the crossroads. Jung called these patterns archetypes. Living images that shape how we love, fear, and search for meaning. When something stirs inside you that feels larger than your own life, connecting you to every person who has grieved or hoped, you are touching the collective unconscious. Not because someone taught you. Because it lives in the bones. What ancient pattern do you feel stirring in your life right now?
