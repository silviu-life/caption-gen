There is a version of you that was buried a long time ago. Not because it was bad. Because someone made you feel it was dangerous. Maybe your intensity was called too much. Maybe your fire was met with silence or punishment. So you learned. You tucked that part away so carefully you forgot it was there. But Jung knew something most people never discover. What you bury does not die. It moves underground. It shows up as the tightness in your chest when someone tells you to relax. As the restlessness that follows you into every room. As the quiet voice at three in the morning whispering, this is not who you really are. The version of you buried in childhood is still alive. Still waiting. Not to be fixed. To be welcomed home. What did you learn to hide about yourself when you were young?
