There is a version of you that you are still punishing. Not who you are now. The one you were. The one who made choices you would not make today. You carry them like a debt you can never repay. Jung understood something about shame most people miss. It disguises itself as honesty. It says I am just being realistic. When in truth it is a wound wearing the mask of wisdom. That person did not have what you have now. Not your awareness or your language for pain. They were surviving with the tools they had. You do not owe the world a perfect past. You owe yourself the mercy of knowing you did what you could. Let this be the day the trial ends. Not because the verdict changed. But because you stopped being both the judge and the accused. Follow if this spoke to something you carry.
