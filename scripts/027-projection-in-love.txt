Have you ever fallen completely in love with someone only to wake up months later wondering who they actually are? Jung had a name for this. He called it Anima projection, the moment when we take the unlived parts of our own soul and drape them over another person like a veil. We do not fall in love with who they are. We fall in love with who we need them to be. The woman who seems to carry all the tenderness you never allowed yourself. The man who radiates the strength you were taught to suppress. They are not that person. They are a mirror, reflecting back the parts of yourself you have not yet claimed. This is why love so often turns to disappointment. Not because the other person changed. But because the projection faded, and you were finally seeing them for the first time. Jung believed real love only begins in that moment of landing. When the veil drops and you choose the real person standing in front of you. Not your fantasy. Not your missing piece. But another whole, imperfect human being. What part of yourself have you been looking for in someone else?
