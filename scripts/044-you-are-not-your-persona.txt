There is a particular kind of exhaustion that comes not from doing too much but from being someone too long. You took on a role, maybe years ago. The dependable one. The one who never needs anything. And it worked. People praised you for it. They called it your identity. But Jung noticed something unsettling. The stronger the Persona grew, the more the real person behind it withered. Like a house no one lives in anymore. The lights are on but something essential has gone quiet inside. You may not notice until someone asks you a simple question. What do you actually want? And you realize you have no idea. The exhaustion you feel is not from what you are doing. It is from who you are not being. What have you stopped doing since the role took over?
