The life you did not live does not disappear. It goes underground. It waits. Jung warned that what we refuse to face does not dissolve. It grows in the dark. Think of the dream you abandoned because someone called it foolish. The part of yourself you buried because it was not rewarded. These unlived possibilities do not stay quiet. They surface as restlessness you cannot name. As anger out of proportion. As envy toward people living the life you were afraid to claim. Jung called this the shadow. And the shadow does not weaken when you ignore it. It gets louder. The unlived life is the most expensive thing you will ever carry. Not because of what it costs you. But because of what it costs everyone around you. The good news is that it is never too late to turn toward what you left behind. The shadow does not punish you for being late. It has been waiting all along. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
