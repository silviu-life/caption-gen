Jung once said the cave you fear to enter holds the treasure you seek. Most people think it sounds poetic. He meant it literally. The thing you are most afraid to face inside yourself is exactly where your power is hiding. The grief you will not touch holds your capacity for deep love. The anger you keep swallowing holds your ability to protect what matters. The ambition you buried because someone called it selfish holds the engine that could build the life you actually want. We are taught to run from darkness. But Jung understood the gold does not sit on the mountaintop. It sits in the depths. You locked your most powerful qualities in the darkest room because once, long ago, someone made you believe those qualities were dangerous. They were not dangerous. They were yours. What is the cave you have been refusing to enter?
