The world tells you to act. To fix. To fill every silence with a solution. But there is a strength that looks like stillness. Jung understood that the Self does not rush. It does not perform. It simply is. Sometimes the most courageous thing you can do is sit with someone in their pain without trying to take it away. Sometimes the greatest act of love is simply staying. We have forgotten that presence is a gift. That being still when everything screams to move is not passivity but the deepest attention you can offer. When was the last time you let yourself be still without calling it laziness?
