Most people are living a story that was handed to them before they could speak. Jung called these complexes. Emotional blueprints written in childhood that dictate adult life long after the original authors have forgotten what they said. A mother who whispers you are too much. A father whose silence teaches you that your needs are a burden. These messages do not vanish when you grow up. They go underground. They become the voice that says do not bother, do not try, do not ask for what you want because people will only leave. And the most dangerous thing about a life script is that it does not feel like a script. It feels like truth. It feels like your own honest assessment of who you are. But it was never yours. It was planted in you before you had the language to question it. The first step toward freedom is not rewriting the story. It is realizing you have been reading someone else's all along. What did they tell you about yourself that you never thought to question?
