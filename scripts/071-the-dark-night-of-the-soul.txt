There comes a time in some lives when everything that once held meaning simply collapses. The job still exists. The relationships are there. But the feeling underneath has drained away, and what remains is an emptiness no effort can fill. Jung called this a confrontation with the Self. The mystics called it the dark night of the soul. Nearly everyone who goes through it believes something has gone terribly wrong. But the collapse of meaning is not the end of the story. It is the beginning of a deeper one. Everything you built in the first half of life was shaped by what others expected. The face you wore. The version of success you chased. Then one day, the psyche says enough. Not because you failed. Because you succeeded at becoming someone you were never meant to be. The dark night is the soul clearing the ground for something more honest to grow. You cannot think your way through it. You can only sit in the darkness long enough for your eyes to adjust. And when they do, you see that what felt like an ending was actually an invitation. The meaning you lost was borrowed. The meaning that comes next will be yours. What did you have to lose before you found what was really yours?
