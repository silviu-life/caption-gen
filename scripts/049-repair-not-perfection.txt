What if the goal was never to get it right? What if the goal was always to come back? We carry this belief that healing means reaching a place where the old fears stop. Where we become someone who does not make the same mistake twice. But Jung saw individuation differently. Not a straight line toward perfection but a spiral. You will meet the same wound again. You will stumble into the same pattern. And the measure of your growth is not whether you fall. It is how quickly you notice. How gently you return. How honestly you say I left, and I am back. There is a trust that only comes from imperfect consistency. Not from someone who never fails you but from someone who always returns. The first person who needs to learn that is you. Not perfection. Repair. What are you willing to come back to today?
