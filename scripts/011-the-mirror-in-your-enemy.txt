The people you call your enemies are often your most honest mirrors. That is not a comfortable truth. But Jung built his understanding of the psyche around it. The qualities we despise most in others are almost always qualities we have disowned in ourselves. The controlling person you cannot stand may mirror your own need to manage everything because trust feels impossible. The person whose dishonesty enrages you may reflect the small lies you tell yourself to keep your self-image intact. We do not hate what is foreign to us. We hate what is familiar but forbidden. Jung called it the projection of the Shadow. Until you look at your enemy and ask what are you showing me about myself, you will keep finding the same person in different faces for the rest of your life. What does the person who bothers you most reveal about what you have hidden?
