What if the goal was never to become a better person? What if it was to become a whole one? Perfection asks you to cut away everything dark. Wholeness asks you to make room for all of it. Jung called this destination the Self. Not the polished version you show the world. The full one. The one that holds your jealousy and your generosity in the same breath. We are taught that growth means removing what is broken. But those parts are not flaws. They are exiles. And they have been waiting to come home. The person who only shows kindness is not whole. They are performing. Wholeness means sitting with contradictions you cannot resolve. But it is the only thing that brings the quiet you have been chasing. What part of yourself have you been trying to cut away instead of welcome home?
