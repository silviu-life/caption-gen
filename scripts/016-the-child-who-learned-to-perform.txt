Some children learn very early that love is not free. It comes with terms. Be good. Be quiet. Be useful. And if you are none of those things, love simply leaves the room. Jung understood this pattern deeply. He saw that when a child is only loved for what they do, they slowly forget who they are. The performance becomes the self. And the real self, the messy, spontaneous, alive self, gets tucked away in a place no one can reach. Years pass. The child becomes an adult who cannot rest without guilt. Who cannot receive a compliment without suspicion. Who measures every relationship by what they are bringing to the table. It looks like ambition. It looks like strength. But underneath it is a small voice still whispering, am I enough yet. The healing begins when you recognize the difference between being loved for your presence and being rewarded for your performance. One of those fed your soul. The other just fed your survival.
