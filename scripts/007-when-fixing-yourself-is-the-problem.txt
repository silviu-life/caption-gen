What if the constant need to fix yourself is the very thing keeping you broken? You spent years reading the books, naming the wounds. And still the same fear sits in your chest. Jung saw the ego is clever at turning even healing into a project. You start wanting to understand your pain. Before long you are optimizing it. Treating your shadow like a problem to engineer away. But the shadow does not respond to project management. It responds to presence. The parts of you that hurt do not need a framework. They need to be sat with. They need you to stop fixing and simply be there. This is what Jung meant by making the unconscious conscious. Not analyzing it. Meeting it with attention that has no agenda. What if the most healing thing you could do is stop trying to heal?
