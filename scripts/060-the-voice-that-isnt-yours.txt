How do you know if the voice in your head is actually yours? This is one of the most unsettling questions in Jungian psychology. Because Jung discovered that much of what we call our inner voice is not ours at all. It is a collection of borrowed instructions. A father's disappointment that became your inner critic. A mother's anxiety that became your fear of risk. These voices do not announce themselves as foreign. They speak in first person. They say I am not good enough and you believe it is your honest assessment. They say I do not deserve this and it feels like wisdom rather than wound. Jung called these complexes. Autonomous fragments of the psyche carrying the emotional charge of the original experience. The work of individuation begins with a strange practice. Learning to hear the voice and ask, is this mine? Or was it installed before I had the power to refuse it? Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
