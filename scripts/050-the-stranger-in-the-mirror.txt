One day, if you are honest enough, you will meet a stranger. Not in the world. In the mirror. Someone with a fire in their eyes you spent your whole life trying to put out. Someone with opinions you trained yourself never to speak. Jung called this the encounter with the Shadow. He said it was one of the most terrifying experiences a person can have. Not because the Shadow is evil. But because it carries everything you sacrificed to be acceptable. Every time you swallowed your anger to keep the peace, it went to the Shadow. Every time you dimmed your light because someone said you were too much, it went to the Shadow. Every time you chose safety over truth, the Shadow kept the receipt. Now it stands behind your reflection, holding everything you abandoned, asking one question. Do you want it back? Most people look away. But Jung knew the stranger in the mirror is more real than the face you show the world. Because the face was built from fear. The stranger was built from truth. And the only way to become whole is to stop turning away. Follow to continue the journey inward.
