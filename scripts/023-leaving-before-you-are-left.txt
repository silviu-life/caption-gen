There is a kind of leaving that happens before anyone actually goes. A quiet withdrawal. A cooling of the heart. A decision made in the body long before the mind finds its excuse. Jung understood that when intimacy deepens beyond a certain point, it triggers a terror older than the relationship itself. Not the fear that this person will hurt you. But the fear of what happens if you stay fully open and they leave anyway. So you leave first. You find the flaw. You create the distance. And you feel a strange relief. But the wound that made you leave follows you into the next relationship and the next. Until you sit still long enough to realize that what you were running from was never the other person. It was the unbearable risk of being fully known. What would happen if you stayed long enough to find out?
