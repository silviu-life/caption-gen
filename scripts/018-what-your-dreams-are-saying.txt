There is a voice that speaks to you every night. It does not use words the way your waking mind does. It uses images. Symbols. Feelings that linger long after you open your eyes. Carl Jung believed that dreams are not random noise. They are letters from the unconscious, written in the only language it knows. And most of us never learn to read them. We dismiss the strange figures and impossible landscapes. But Jung saw the psyche trying to restore balance. When you ignore a feeling during the day, it visits you at night dressed in a symbol you cannot look away from. When you refuse to grieve, the dream gives you water, floods, oceans you cannot cross. When you have locked away a part of yourself, it sends a stranger knocking at a door. Your unconscious does not speak to punish you. It speaks because it has been waiting, sometimes for years, for you to listen. The next time you wake from a dream that unsettles you, do not rush to forget it. Sit with it. Ask it what it came to say. You may find it has been saying the same thing for a very long time. Follow for more from the depths of the psyche.
